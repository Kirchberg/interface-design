# Craft Critique Protocol

Use this after a first UI implementation, before claiming the result is final.

## Composition

- Does the screen have one clear focal point?
- Do dense areas give way to breathing room?
- Are proportions making a statement about priority?
- Does the page rhythm vary intentionally, or is every card/gap/section the same?

## Craft

- Are surfaces establishing quiet hierarchy?
- Are borders subtle enough to disappear when not inspected?
- Is typography distinguishable by weight, opacity, and spacing, not just size?
- Do controls respond to hover, press, focus, disabled, and loading states?
- Are layout values on the spacing grid?

## Content

- Does the visible copy sound like one real product?
- Could a real person or company plausibly be looking at this data?
- Are metrics, labels, empty states, and actions coherent?

## Structure

- Are negative margins, absolute positioning, and `calc()` hacks hiding layout issues?
- Can the layout be explained with normal flow, grid, flex, max-width, and reusable components?
- Are reusable values tokenized?

## Final Question

Ask: "If the user said this lacks craft, what would they point to?"

Fix that before presenting the work.
